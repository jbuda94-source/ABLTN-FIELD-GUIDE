Session View — the grid

Live session view — interactive

Session View is a grid of clips. Each column is a Track (Drums, Bass, Synth). Each row is a Scene. You launch clips freely — they loop and you can trigger any combination live.

This is your sketchpad. Not a song — a playground. Jam here until something feels right, then record it into Arrangement View.

Try it: Drag a loop from the browser (Cmd+Alt+B) into any clip slot. Press the triangle to launch. Press the square to stop. That's the whole idea.

Arrangement View — the timeline

Arrangement View is the traditional DAW timeline. Time moves left to right. You place clips in a fixed order to build a complete song from intro to outro.

Press TAB to switch between Session and Arrangement at any time. Most producers sketch in Session, then record into Arrangement by jamming clips while recording is active.

Shortcut: In Arrangement View, press Cmd R to start recording. Play your Session clips live — Ableton captures your performance to the timeline.

MIDI vs Audio

MIDI
Note data — not sound. Tells an instrument what to play. Swap the synth and the whole vibe changes.
Audio
Actual recorded sound. Drag in a WAV or MP3. Warp mode syncs it to your BPM.
Rule of thumb: If you want to change the notes, use MIDI. If you want to use a recording or a sample, use Audio.

Signal chain — how sound flows

Track device chain

Every track has a device chain. Sound flows left to right through each device. Order matters — a compressor before reverb sounds different than reverb before compressor.

Drag instruments and effects from the browser into any track. Right-click a device to delete, bypass, or reorder it.

Start simple: Add EQ Eight to every track. Just cut everything below 80Hz on non-bass tracks. That alone cleans up a mix dramatically.

Tempo & the grid

BPM genre reference

The BPM field lives top-left. Click and drag up/down to change it, or click and type a number. Everything you record locks to it.

The grid divides time into bars and beats. Most electronic music is in 4/4 — four beats per bar. Clips snap to the grid by default, keeping everything in time.

Shortcut: Right-click the arrangement to change grid size. Hold Cmd while dragging to go off-grid.

Built-in instruments — don't sleep on these

Drift
Analog-style synth. Great for basses, warm pads, leads. Start with presets.
Wavetable
Modern wavetable synth. Evolving textures, leads, complex sounds.
Drum Rack
Pad-based beat machine. Drop samples onto pads. One pad per sound.
Simpler
Drag any audio sample in, play it chromatically. Easiest sampler there is.
Advice: Don't buy third-party plugins yet. Master these four first — they're what a lot of professionals actually use.

First session

check off as you go
Session progress
0 / 6
1
Create a new Live Set
Open Ableton → File → New Live Set. You'll land in Session View — a blank grid. This is your canvas. Don't be overwhelmed.
2
Set your BPM
Find the tempo field top-left. Click and type a number. Try 128 for house, 90 for hip-hop. Everything locks to this — choose something and commit.
3
Find a drum loop in the browser
Press Cmd Alt B (Mac) or Ctrl Alt B (PC) to open the browser. Go to Packs → Core Library → Drums. Drag a loop into a clip slot on an Audio track. Press the triangle to launch it.
4
Add a synth on a MIDI track
Click + to add a MIDI track. Drag Drift from the Instruments browser onto it. Double-click an empty clip slot to open the piano roll — draw a few notes. Close it and press play.
5
Launch both clips and listen
Launch your drum clip and synth clip. They'll loop in sync. Don't try to make something good — just listen, adjust notes, try different loop lengths. Get used to the loop running.
6
Save — and collect all files
Press Cmd S (Mac) or Ctrl S (PC). When prompted to collect and save, say yes. This embeds all samples into the project. Do this every session.
Real talk: Your first loop will probably sound terrible. That's fine — every great producer has hundreds of bad loops in their project folders. The goal right now is to get comfortable with the tools, not to make a hit.

What to do next

week 1 & month 1

🟡 This week

  • Add EQ Eight to every track, cut below 80Hz on non-bass
  • Try Drum Rack — build a beat from individual samples
  • Learn Warp mode to sync any audio to your BPM
  • Record automation: right-click any knob → Edit Automation

🔵 This month

  • Learn sidechain compression — the pumping effect in house/techno
  • Group tracks (Cmd+G) and use Return Tracks for shared reverb
  • Export your first full track: File → Export Audio/Video
  • Post it somewhere and get feedback — Discord, SoundCloud
